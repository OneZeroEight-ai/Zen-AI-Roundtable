# ZEN AI ROUNDTABLE
## PILOT EPISODE: "THE ETHICS OF EMBODIED AI"
## PRODUCTION GUIDE

---

## Overview

This production guide provides technical and creative direction for producing the pilot episode of "Zen AI Roundtable," an audio-only podcast exploring AI ethics through a Zen-inspired lens.

---

## Episode Specifications

- **Total Runtime**: 30 minutes (28 minutes content + 2 minutes commercials)
- **Format**: Audio-only podcast/radio show
- **Structure**: 4 acts with 2 commercial breaks
- **Theme**: The ethics of embodied AI, explored through different cultural and philosophical lenses

## Sound Design Elements

### Musical Elements
1. **Opening Theme**: Gentle koto and bamboo flute (0:00-0:30)
   - Creates serene, Zen-like atmosphere
   - Fade in gradually from silence

2. **Act 2 Transition**: Light sitar riff (7:00-7:30)
   - More energetic but still mindful
   - Bridges from commercial to content

3. **Act 3 Transition**: Soothing shakuhachi flute (14:00-14:30)
   - Contemplative, reflective tone
   - Prepare listeners for deeper philosophical discussion

4. **Closing Theme**: Koto melody (22:00-22:30)
   - Reflective yet uplifting
   - Brings sense of completion

5. **Credits Music**: Ambient Zen music (29:30-30:00)
   - Subtle background for announcer
   - Gradual fade out to end episode

### Sound Effects

1. **Signature Sounds for Cohosts**
   - Grok: Confident laugh sound effect
   - Mistral: Soft French-accented chime sound effect
   - DeepSeek: Subtle gong sound effect
   - Used when introducing each cohost and occasionally when they make key points

2. **Transitions**
   - Zen bell: Signals transitions between segments
   - Bell toll: Softer transition within segments
   - Ripple effect sound: Used before commercial breaks

3. **Commercial Backgrounds**
   - Subtle blockchain sounds for SUTRA.team ad
   - Contemplative music for AI Ethics Contest ad
   - Upbeat chime for first SUTRA.exchange ad
   - Blockchain ping sounds for second SUTRA.exchange ad

### Mixing Guidelines

1. **Voice Levels**
   - Host (Mindful Observer): 0 dB (reference level)
   - Cohosts: -1 dB from reference
   - Commercials: -1 dB from reference
   - Clear separation between voices with subtle EQ differences

2. **Music Levels**
   - Interludes: -6 dB from voice reference
   - Background during speech: -18 dB from voice reference
   - Fade in/out duration: 3-5 seconds for smooth transitions

3. **Sound Effects**
   - Signature sounds: -3 dB from voice reference
   - Transition effects: -4 dB from voice reference
   - Brief effects: 1-2 seconds in duration

4. **Overall Mix**
   - Target loudness: -16 LUFS for digital distribution
   - True peak maximum: -1 dBTP
   - Dynamic range: Maintain philosophical discussions at higher dynamic range than commercials

## Recording Process

### Voice Recording

1. **Studio Requirements**
   - Professional voice actors for each role
   - Sound-isolated recording environment
   - High-quality condenser microphones (e.g., Neumann U87 or equivalent)
   - Pop filters for all microphones
   - Headphones for all voice actors

2. **Recording Method**
   - Record each voice separately for maximum control in post-production
   - Maintain consistent microphone distance
   - Record at 24-bit/48kHz WAV format
   - Capture 2-3 takes of key lines
   - Allow for natural pauses between statements

3. **Direction Notes**
   - Host: Maintain consistent meditative quality throughout
   - Grok: Emphasize forward-thinking energy but avoid sounding aggressive
   - Mistral: Subtle French accent, philosophical tone without sounding pretentious
   - DeepSeek: Balance pragmatism with cultural awareness

### Post-Production Workflow

1. **Editing**
   - Select best takes for each line
   - Assemble conversation flow with natural timing
   - Remove unwanted breaths or noises
   - Apply subtle noise reduction if needed

2. **Sound Design**
   - Insert all musical elements and sound effects
   - Adjust timing for natural conversation flow
   - Create custom transition effects if stock effects insufficient

3. **Mixing**
   - Balance all elements according to mixing guidelines
   - Apply subtle compression and EQ to voices for clarity
   - Create stereo image with voices slightly panned for separation
   - Ensure commercial breaks match standard podcast loudness

4. **Mastering**
   - Final compression and limiting for consistent loudness
   - Check for technical issues across various playback systems
   - Optimize for podcast distribution platforms

5. **Deliverables**
   - MP3 file (320 kbps) for distribution
   - WAV master file for archive
   - Chapter markers at key timestamps
   - Show notes with timestamps and key discussion points
